Questions

Will my Chrome extension actually work?

Most do. Viaduct rewrites the manifest and shims the chrome.* APIs Safari lacks. Before converting, it reports any incompatibilities so there are no silent surprises. Some things — like OAuth flows that depend on chrome.identity — can't be fixed by conversion alone, and Viaduct tells you honestly when that's the case.

Do I need to know how to code?

No. User mode is a single drop zone — drop an extension, get it in Safari. You'll need Xcode and Node.js installed (both free) because Safari requires a signed, built app extension; Viaduct drives all of that for you.

Why does it keep my battery better than Chrome?

The converted extension runs inside Safari's own engine instead of a separate Chromium process humming in the background. One less browser engine running means less CPU and longer battery life.

What's the difference between Free and Pro?

Free is the full app — you just get 2 conversions, and they aren't auto-resigned (so they lapse in about a week, like any free Apple signature). Pro lifts the cap to unlimited and turns on auto-resigning so your extensions never silently disappear. Pro is a one-time $19 purchase — yours forever, updates included.

Why isn't this on the Mac App Store?

Conversion shells out to node and xcodebuild, which the App Store sandbox forbids. So Viaduct ships as a notarized direct download instead — same Apple notarization, just outside the store.
